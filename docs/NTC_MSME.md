# Why FinHealth Card Works for NTC MSMEs

**NTC (New-To-Credit)** MSMEs have **no or thin commercial bureau history** and often **no audited financial statements** — yet many run viable businesses visible through **digital exhaust**.

## The problem

| Traditional lender asks | NTC MSME reality |
|-------------------------|------------------|
| 2–3 years audited P&L | Maintains informal books |
| Commercial CIBIL / rank | No borrowings → no score |
| Collateral documentation | Asset-light operations |
| Bank statement analysis | Multiple informal accounts |

**Result:** Automatic decline despite healthy GST filings, UPI collections, and payroll.

## How alt-data closes the gap

FinHealth Card builds a **financial health view** from behaviour proxies:

```
GST compliance + turnover trend  →  Revenue quality
UPI merchant volume              →  Collections (retail/services)
AA bank data                     →  Liquidity & EMI discipline
EPFO payroll                     →  Employment & wage stability
Promoter bureau                  →  Character (where available)
Litigation + sentiment           →  Governance & reputation
```

## Business benefits

### For the bank
- **Expand addressable market** — MSME segment underserved by scorecards
- **Faster TAT** — Minutes vs days (consent + fetch + score)
- **Lower acquisition cost** — No physical document collection for many signals
- **Explainable declines** — Pillar breakdown reduces regulatory friction

### For the borrower
- **Fair access** — Credit for businesses with digital track record
- **No audited CA dependency** for initial limit
- **Transparent feedback** — Flags show what to improve (GST compliance, etc.)

### Risk mitigation
- **Cross-validation** — GST turnover vs UPI vs bank credits
- **Fraud signals** — Mismatch between electricity and declared production
- **Promoter overlay** — Thin commercial file supplemented by consumer bureau
- **Early warning** — Filing delays, bounces, litigation before default

## NTC vs established MSME

| Dimension | Established MSME | NTC MSME |
|-----------|------------------|----------|
| Primary signal | Bureau + financials | Alt-data mosaic |
| Weight on GST/UPI | Supplementary | **Core** |
| Promoter bureau | Business score | **Consumer** promoter score |
| Decision speed | Standard | **Near real-time** |
| FinHealth Card fit | Enhancement | **Primary path** |

## Regulatory alignment (India)

- **Account Aggregator** — RBI-regulated consent-based data sharing
- **GST** — Authorised GSP access with taxpayer consent
- **DPDP Act** — Consent and purpose limitation in production
- **RBI fair practices** — Explainable credit decisions supported by pillar view

## Elevator pitch

> Traditional credit asks for a **history the NTC MSME doesn't have**. FinHealth Card asks for a **digital footprint they already create** — tax filings, payments, payroll, and bank behaviour — and turns it into an explainable credit decision in minutes.
